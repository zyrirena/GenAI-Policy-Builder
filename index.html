Key Compliance Landscape

⚡
EU AI Act (2024) — Mandatory Risk Classification

The EU AI Act introduces four risk tiers: Unacceptable, High, Limited, and Minimal. Many enterprise GenAI uses in hiring, credit, critical infrastructure, and law enforcement are classified HIGH RISK, requiring mandatory conformity assessments, human oversight, and documentation obligations before market placement.

🔒
Data Privacy — GDPR · CCPA · HIPAA

GenAI agents processing personal data of EU residents (GDPR), California residents (CCPA), or health-related information (HIPAA) face significant obligations including data minimization, purpose limitation, the right to explanation for automated decisions, and potentially a DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment).

⚖️
Anti-Discrimination & Employment Law

AI agents used in hiring, performance management, lending, or housing decisions are subject to anti-discrimination laws (EEOC Title VII, ECOA, FHA). The EEOC has issued guidance on AI and automated systems in employment. Bias testing and adverse impact analysis are legally prudent and increasingly required.

✓
NIST AI RMF — Voluntary but Widely Adopted

The NIST AI RMF (Jan 2023) is voluntary but referenced by federal agencies, regulators, and enterprise procurement requirements. Aligning to it demonstrates due diligence, reduces liability exposure, and is rapidly becoming a de facto standard for enterprise AI governance.

INCIDENT SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION
CRITICAL — Immediate Action Required

AI causes or enables direct harm to a person; unauthorized access to regulated data; major public safety or legal violation; system used for clearly illegal purpose

HIGH — Same Business Day Response

Systemic policy violations; confirmed bias affecting a user group; privacy incident (potential PII exposure); significant reputational risk; multiple user complaints about harmful output

MEDIUM — 48-Hour Response

Isolated policy violation; accuracy below threshold; user complaint with no clear harm; performance degradation; model drift detected
